The Journey

First in my family to leave.
Not the last thing I'll do
that nobody's done before.

"I landed in Dublin in September 2025 with a suitcase, a scholarship, and absolutely no idea how cold it was going to get — in more ways than one."

Growing up in India, I had every convenience. Family around the corner. Friends always available. A life built on warmth and proximity. Moving to Ireland meant dismantling all of that and rebuilding from scratch — alone, in a new country, with a new culture and a whole new set of rules for how to exist.


The first Diwali I spent here, I barely felt it. No lights, no noise, no smell of mithai in the house. Just a quiet flat in Dublin and the realisation that belonging is something you have to build, not something that follows you across borders.


What surprised me most wasn't the loneliness. It was how much the hard parts changed me. I started eating right, sleeping properly, cutting out noise. I started showing up for myself the way I'd always shown up for other people. Ireland gave me the independence to become more fully myself.


My parents made this possible. Their hard work, their belief, their sacrifice — that weight rides with me every day. It doesn't feel like pressure. It feels like fuel.

Life · Vol. 01

Nobody Told Me Living Fully Was This Exhausting — And I Wouldn't Have It Any Other Way

Everyone talks about "living life to the fullest" like it's a vibe, a filter, a caption. Nobody tells you it's actually a practice — and a pretty demanding one.

I moved to Dublin at 22 having never lived outside India. I came here to study, yes, but also to find out what I was made of when the usual scaffolding wasn't there. No family down the road. No childhood friends to call. No shortcut back to comfort.

What I found was this: living fully isn't about doing more. It's about being more present for what you're already doing. It's waking up and choosing to engage rather than coast. It's eating well not because you're supposed to, but because you've learned what it does to your energy. It's putting your phone down because you realised you were missing the actual moments.

It's also, I'll be honest, job applications and near misses and days where you wonder if any of it is working. It's the very specific exhaustion of carrying big expectations — your own and others'. That part doesn't make the highlight reel, but it's where the real growth happens.

The sports I've played across my life taught me something that no classroom has — you only find out what you're capable of when you're genuinely tested. Living fully is just the long version of that choice, made over and over again, every single day.

I'm figuring it out. But I'm enjoying the process. And I think that's enough for now.

March 2026 · Dublin, Ireland
